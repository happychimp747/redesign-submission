General Information on Rhode Island Bar Admissions

The qualifications needed for admission of attorneys and others to practice law are set forth in Article II of the Rhode Supreme Court Rules. Generally, admission to the Rhode Island Bar requires passing the Rhode Island Bar Examination. In Article II the Supreme Court has created limited exceptions to the requirement that only Rhode Island attorneys who have passed the Rhode Island Bar Examination can practice law in Rhode Island. There are several types of admissions for the Rhode Island Bar:

  • Rule 1
    • (a)Admission on Examination View detailed information
    • (b)Admission on Transferred Uniform Bar Examination Score View detailed information
  • Rule 2: Attorney Admitted in Other
    • (a): On Examination
    • (b) and (e): Programs providing services to indigents - Law school faculty supervising clinical law programs
    • (c) and (d): Admission while on active duty with the armed services - Judge Advocate Generals
  • Rule 9 Nonresident attorneys
      Pro Hac Vice Admission Registration of In-house Counsel
  • Rule 10
      Professional Service Corporations, Limited Liability Partnerships, and Limited Liability Companies (Limited Liability Entities)
  • Rule 11: Legal Services Organizations
